When we say «touch» we may actually refer to different things, is the pain felt the same way as cold ? Do we feel force with the same mechanisms as we feel the softness of a blanket ?

Let’s talk about how everything works, but first let’s introduce some words.

The somatosensory system is a part of the sensory nervous system, it responds to changes at the surface or inside of the body.

The somatic senses or somesthetic senses are the sense of touch, proprioception (sense of position and movement) and haptic perception. Haptic perception is often associated with active touch.

And because that’s not enough term, engineer often talks about Haptic feedback being divided in kinesthetic and tactile.

Still following ? lets try to get into how it works.

All sharing the same channel

Gate Control Theory

running cool water on a burned area = less pain

Tactile receptors are cells reacting to tactile stimilus, they can be divide in thre types.

Interestingly they sort of use the same channel to convey the information to the brain.

This create an effect called gating, information are competing to get to the brain.

And that’s a why running your finger under cool water diminish the pain sensation associated with a burn.
